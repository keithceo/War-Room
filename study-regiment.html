⚡ EXECUTION RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE
01
Active recall beats passive reading every time. Don't re-read your notes for the fifth time. Close them, test yourself, then check. That gap between what you thought you knew and what you actually know — that's where marks are won.
02
Night sessions = consolidation only. Never introduce new content at midnight. Your brain consolidates during those hours, not absorbs. New material before sleep = poor retention. Use nights for practice and recall drills.
03
FM 3A is your highest-risk module. Calculations are unforgiving. One wrong formula = cascading errors. Allocate 30% more time here than any other module. Accuracy at speed is the goal.
04
Breaks are part of the schedule, not a reward. Build in 20–30 min breaks after every 90-minute session. Pomodoro logic: your focus is a finite resource. Manage it like capital.
05
Track your mock exam scores daily from Day 3. Create a simple score log. Rising scores = momentum. Flat or declining scores = immediate intervention. You can't manage what you don't measure.
06
Leverage your existing BM 3A work. Your Pick n Pay and Versus Socks assignment frameworks are already exam-quality material. Don't rebuild — refine and weaponise them.
07
Law of Concentrated Force (Law 23): In the final 3 days, study only what you're getting wrong. Don't waste time re-studying what you know. Concentration of effort on weak points is the only path from 70% to 90%.
